What does confidence % mean?
A 62% confidence score in a 3-way matchup means the AI calculates a 62% probability its pick beats the other two candidates in this specific comparison.
Strong advantage
A random guess among 3 candidates would average 33.3% each. 62% is nearly double that baseline — a real, meaningful edge, not a marginal one.
Not a guarantee
62% still leaves a 38% chance one of the other two candidates wins instead.
Relative, not absolute
This measures how the pick compares only to the other two candidates in that matchup — it's not a prediction that the stock's price will rise, only that it was judged the strongest of the three presented.
Where the remaining 38% goes
It splits between the two runners-up depending on how close the matchup was — e.g. a clear runner-up might get 30% (with 8% left for last place), or a tighter three-way call might split closer to 19%/19%.
Why "Top 3 for Diversification" scores don't sum to 100%
Each of the three round-1 group winners came from its own separate 3-way matchup against two different stocks (not shown) — three independent comparisons, not one shared decision. So their confidence scores have no reason to add up to 100% across the three of them; that math only applies within a single matchup. It's also normal for the same stock to show a different confidence number in "The Pick" than in "Top 3" if it wins both rounds — the final round is a fresh comparison against the other two group winners, a different matchup than the one it won in round 1.
